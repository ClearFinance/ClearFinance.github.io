How to use this calculator?

Clearly define what you are saving for — that determines the time horizon and the right instrument. An emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) should be in something liquid and low-risk. A 5+ year goal can assume more risk and potentially higher returns.

The return rate depends on where you invest: 0% if keeping cash, 3-6% in term deposits or fixed income instruments, 6-10% historically in equity index funds (with higher volatility). For short-term goals (under 2 years), use low rates.

Once you know the monthly amount needed, set it up as an automatic transfer on the day you receive your income — so saving happens before you have the opportunity to spend that money.
