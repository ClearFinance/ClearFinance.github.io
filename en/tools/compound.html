How does this calculator work?

Applies the compound interest formula: FV = PV × (1 + r/n)^(n×t) + PMT × [((1 + r/n)^(n×t) − 1) / (r/n)], where PV is initial capital, PMT the monthly contribution, r the annual rate, n the compounding frequency and t the time in years.

The result shows exactly how much of your final wealth comes from your own money and how much was generated by compound interest alone. The difference tends to surprise — especially over 20+ year horizons.

For context: the S&P 500 has had a historical average return of approximately 10% annually in nominal terms (7% real after inflation). Emerging markets and other assets have different risk/return profiles.
